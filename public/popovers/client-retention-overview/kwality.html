KWALITY HOUSE - Client Conversion Analysis

Top 10 Most Useful Findings

  1. Overall Conversion Rate Declining: Total conversion rate dropped from 42.4% in Jan 2024 to 27.4% in Nov 2025, with a low of 16.5% in Oct 2025, indicating challenges in turning trials into paying members.
  2. Trial Classes Dominate New Members: New - Trial Class accounts for 52-68% of trials monthly (e.g., 68 in Jun 2024) and 49-53% of new members, but conversion rates vary (28.3%-46.3%), suggesting trials are a strong lead source but inconsistent.
  3. Referral Classes Show Steady Growth: New - Referral Class trials increased from 0 in early 2024 to 30 in Dec 2025, with conversion rates rising to 41.2% in Jun 2025, showing referrals are becoming a reliable, low-cost acquisition channel.
  4. High LTV from Paid First Visits: Avg LTV for New - Paid First Visit peaked at ₹33.4K in May 2025, with total LTV of ₹2.0L in May 2025, indicating these clients yield premium value if converted quickly.
  5. Retention Peaks in Mid-Year: Overall retention rate averaged 39.3% in Apr 2025 but dipped to 17.6% in Aug 2025, with New - Referral Class retaining 58.8% in Jun 2025—suggesting seasonal patterns (better in spring/summer).
  6. Teacher Performance Varies Widely: Pranjali Jain led with 23 new members but only 17.4% conversion; Rohan Dahima had 38.5% conversion with 13 new members, highlighting the need for teacher specialization.
  7. Hosted Classes Underperform: In Dec 2025, hosted classes had only 8.3% retention and 8.3% conversion, despite 12 trials, compared to overall 24.5% retention.
  8. Avg Visits Decline Over Time: Total avg visits dropped from 38.3 in Jan 2024 to 3.5 in Dec 2025, signaling reduced engagement post-acquisition.
  9. Dec 2025 Totals Show Recovery: 109 trials led to 106 new members (97.2% trial-to-member rate), 26 retained (24.5%), and ₹6.8L total LTV, a slight uptick from Nov 2025 lows.
  10. Client Type "New - Others" Has High Variability: Avg LTV spiked at ₹2.4L in Apr 2024 but fell to ₹1.6K in Nov 2025, with conversion rates from 0% to 100%, indicating inconsistent handling of miscellaneous leads.

10 Insights

  1. Seasonal Trends in Trials: Trials peak in summer (e.g., 126 in Jul 2025) and dip in winter (e.g., 10 in Jan 2026), correlating with fitness resolutions, but conversion lags behind, suggesting follow-up timing issues.
  2. LTV Inversely Correlates with Conversion Speed: High LTV clients (e.g., ₹1.6L for Studio Annual Unlimited) take longer to convert (avg 2 days), while low-LTV ones (e.g., ₹860 for No Membership Purchase - Trial Class) convert faster but yield less revenue.
  3. Referral Retention Outperforms Trials: New - Referral Class has 37.5%-66.7% retention vs. 23.3%-46.9% for New - Trial Class, implying referrals bring more loyal clients due to personal endorsements.
  4. Teacher Conversion Rates Tied to Sessions: Teachers with more sessions (e.g., Reshma Sharma with 353 sessions) have 30% conversion but lower retention (30%), suggesting over-reliance on volume over quality.
  5. Hosted Classes Fail to Convert Trials: Only 1 out of 12 hosted class trials converted in Dec 2025, with 0 retention for Raman's Barre, pointing to event-specific issues like instructor fit or promotion.
  6. Paid First Visits Have Short Avg Days: Avg conv days of 1-48 for New - Paid First Visit indicate quick decisions, but retention drops to 35.3% in Dec 2025, showing impulse buys don't stick.
  7. Unknown Client Type Anomalies: "Unknown" category has erratic LTV (₹5.4L in Sep 2024) and visits (55 in Oct 2024), likely data entry errors masking potential high-value segments.
  8. Dec 2025 Avg Visits Low Across Types: Even retained clients averaged 3.5 visits, down from 38.3 in Jan 2024, suggesting post-pandemic fatigue or competition from home workouts.
  9. Influencer Sign-Ups Underutilized: Only 1-8 trials monthly, with 100% conversion in Mar 2024 but 0% retention, indicating influencers drive initial interest but not loyalty.
  10. Total LTV Declines Post-2024 Peak: From ₹16.2L in Jan 2024 to ₹3.7K in Jan 2026, with New - Trial Class contributing 40-60% of LTV, signaling market saturation or pricing pressures.
