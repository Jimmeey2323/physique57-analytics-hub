📈 Monthly Trend

January brought a remarkable 252 new clients — driven largely by the Peakst8 outdoor class series — but converted at just 6.0%. This reflects that outdoor/hosted events attract volume but require a nurture bridge to the studio. February and March show a strong and encouraging pattern: as outdoor class volume normalised, conversion rates improved dramatically — reaching 19.4% in March, the highest of the quarter. This is the healthy shape of a maturing funnel: volume drops, quality rises. March's trajectory points to a much stronger Q2.

📈
March Conversion Rate Is a Breakout Signal
March's 19.4% conversion rate is nearly 3× January's rate — and is the highest single-month conversion rate at this location all quarter. As the new client mix shifts from bulk outdoor events toward more targeted studio trials, the funnel is maturing rapidly.
🏃
Outdoor / Peakst8 Programme — Massive Brand Reach
The Peakst8 and Avg Run Club partnerships brought 200+ new clients through outdoor activations — a unique, community-first acquisition channel not replicated elsewhere in the network. This is an extraordinary brand-building asset that seeds future studio members.
💡
Outdoor Leads Need a Studio Conversion Bridge
280 hosted/outdoor class visitors converted at just 0.4–4.3%. These clients enjoy the brand in a low-commitment setting. A structured 'Come to the Studio' incentive — e.g. a ₹500 first studio class offer — sent within 48 hours of an outdoor event is the missing link in the funnel.
💰
Paid First Visit Has the Strongest Conversion Here
40% conversion and 60% retention for paid first visit clients — the highest at this location. With a growing member base becoming aware of the studio, promoting a paid first visit product to outdoor event participants could be transformative.
🚀
Kajol & Pushyank Drive Hosted Class Volume
Between them, Kajol Kanchan (103) and Pushyank Nahar (94) delivered 197 hosted/outdoor class introductions — extraordinary outreach volume. While their overall conversion rates are below average (partly a reflection of the outdoor class mix), their community-building work is laying the foundation for long-term growth.
📊
'Others' Category Has Surprisingly High Conversion
49 clients tagged as 'Others' converted at 34.7% — the second-highest rate after Paid First Visit. Understanding what this source represents (corporate events? specific campaigns?) will help replicate and scale it deliberately.

Q2 2026 Recommendations
Strategic Action Plan
Prioritised actions to maximise new client conversion and retention heading into Q2.
  • 1
    Build a Studio-to-Outdoor Conversion Bridge: Create a structured follow-up for all outdoor/hosted class participants: a 24-hour personalised message from the attending trainer, a ₹499–₹999 'First Studio Class' offer, and a 7-day reminder. Even converting 5% of the 280 hosted class visitors (14 clients) would add ~₹85K+ in incremental revenue at current LTV rates.
  • 2
    Replicate March's Conversion Performance in Q2: March delivered a 19.4% conversion rate — the best of the quarter and a clear proof point that the team can convert at a high level when the client mix is right. Build on this momentum: document what changed in March (was it a campaign? follow-up change? client mix?), and carry it into April and May.
  • 3
    Scale the Paid First Visit Pathway Through Outdoor Participants: Paid first visits convert at 40% and retain at 60% — the strongest signals in the funnel. Target outdoor event participants with a direct paid-entry offer: 'Loved the outdoor class? Book your first studio session for ₹999.' This bridges the outdoor-to-studio gap in the most commercially efficient way.
  • 4
    Investigate and Scale the 'Others' Source: 49 clients from unspecified 'Others' sources converted at 34.7% — far above the studio average. Understanding what drives this group (specific campaigns, corporate tie-ins, instructor referrals?) will allow the team to replicate and scale this channel deliberately in Q2.
  • 5
    Coach Kajol and Pushyank on Studio Conversion: Both trainers manage the highest new client volumes at the studio, but their conversion rates (7.7% and 7.8%) reflect the outdoor-to-studio challenge rather than poor performance. Equip them with a post-class conversion toolkit — follow-up message templates, studio trial vouchers — so they can bridge the gap from outdoor engagement to studio membership.
