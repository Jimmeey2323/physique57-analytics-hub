Executive Summary: The Engine, The Leak, & The Anomaly

Supreme HQ's November acquisition funnel is a study in contrasts. On one hand, the `Trial Class` channel is operating as a spectacular, high-performance engine, single-handedly responsible for nearly 90% of all new member conversions. However, its success is being diluted by two critical issues: a persistent leak in the form of a high-volume, low-value `Hosted Class` strategy, and a sudden, alarming anomaly—the complete failure of the historically reliable `Referral` channel, which dropped to a 0% conversion rate.

The immediate priorities are twofold: first, investigate and rectify the referral program failure to restore a vital source of warm leads. Second, re-engineer the acquisition strategy to starve the inefficient channels and pour every available resource into amplifying the `Trial Class` and `Paid First Visit` funnels, which are proven to attract clients who are ready and willing to commit.

The 15-Point Blueprint for High-Quality Growth

I. Triage & Diagnosis (The Immediate Fixes)

  1. 1.
    Launch an Immediate Investigation into the Referral Program.

    Rationale: A 0% conversion rate from your warmest leads is a critical failure. Was there a technical glitch in the sign-up process? Did the referral offer change? Interview the 11 referred clients and your team to find the root cause within 7 days. This is your top priority.

  2. 2.
    Place a Moratorium on Most "Hosted Classes".

    Rationale: With a 2.4% conversion rate, this channel is a resource drain. Halt all new bookings except for partners who fit the profile of your one successful event ("Aditi's Barre"). Your studio is not an event space; it's a conversion center.

  3. 3.
    Protect the `Trial Class` Engine at All Costs.

    Rationale: This channel is carrying the entire business. Ensure these trialists are getting into your best classes (like `Studio FIT`) with your best instructors. Their first experience must be flawless to maintain your ~30% conversion rate.

  4. 4.
    Simplify Your "Paid First Visit" Offer.

    Rationale: Only 5 people took this high-converting offer. Is it hard to find on your website? Is the price point confusing? Make it a clear, compelling, can't-miss "First Class Pass" to attract more of these valuable, high-intent clients.

  5. 5.
    Analyze the "Aditi's Barre Hosted Class" Success.

    Rationale: This event converted at 14.3%, 6x the channel average. Deconstruct it. Was Aditi's audience a perfect fit? Was there a special offer for attendees? This is your only successful hosted event template.

II. Funnel Optimization (The Strategic Shift)

  1. 6.
    Pivot Hosted Events to a Paid Corporate Model.

    Rationale: Instead of free events for influencers, sell private class packages to corporations. This pre-qualifies attendees as professionals (higher LTV) and generates guaranteed revenue regardless of conversion.

  2. 7.
    Launch a "Double-Sided" Referral Campaign.

    Rationale: Once the technical/process issues are fixed, relaunch the program with an irresistible offer: the new member gets a discount, and the referrer gets an equal credit. This incentivizes both parties to ensure the trial happens.

  3. 8.
    Create a High-Converting Landing Page for `Trial Class`.

    Rationale: Since this is your main source of business, its digital storefront must be perfect. The page should feature testimonials, highlight the value, and have a frictionless sign-up process.

  4. 9.
    Introduce a `Trial Class` Follow-Up Protocol.

    Rationale: The 6-day conversion window is key. Implement an automated SMS or email 24 hours after their first class with a personal message and a direct link to a "New Member Offer."

  5. 10.
    Focus Digital Ads Exclusively on the `Trial Class` Funnel.

    Rationale: Stop spending money promoting low-conversion offers. Every ad dollar should be directed at getting more people into your proven, high-LTV `Trial Class` pipeline.

III. Systems & Growth (The Flywheel)

  1. 11.
    Define and Track your "Ideal Client" Profile.

    Rationale: Your `Trial Class` and `Paid First Visit` converters are your ideal clients. Survey them. What are their goals? Where do they live? Use this data to refine your marketing targeting.

  2. 12.
    Set Clear Monthly KPIs for Conversions, Not Just Trials.

    Rationale: Shift the team's focus from a vanity metric (trials) to what actually matters (new paying members). The goal isn't "50 trials," it's "15 high-quality conversions."

  3. 13.
    Test a Multi-Class Trial Pack.

    Rationale: Since clients visit 4.4 times before converting, a single trial class might not be enough. Test a paid "3-Class Intro Pack" to increase their immersion and commitment, likely boosting conversion rates further.

  4. 14.
    Implement a Staff Incentive for Conversions.

    Rationale: Offer a small bonus to front desk staff or instructors for each trial client they personally help convert to a full membership. This aligns the entire team around the goal of growth.

  5. 15.
    Review Funnel Metrics Weekly.

    Rationale: The referral program's failure shows how quickly a key channel can break. A brief, 15-minute weekly review of conversion rates will allow you to spot and fix problems before they derail an entire month's growth.
