Section I — Executive Summary

Supreme HQ, Bandra continues to be the highest-volume and highest-absolute-LTV location in the network, welcoming 271 new members across Q1 2026 and generating a cumulative LTV of ₹13.37L for the quarter. This flagship location's performance profile is characterised by strong top-of-funnel intake, high per-converted-member LTV values, and an increasingly diverse multi-channel acquisition mix — with website, WhatsApp, hosted class, and client referral all playing material roles. January led the quarter with ₹6.21L in LTV from 92 new members, building on strong December momentum. February saw new members moderate to 85 while conversions held at 17 and total LTV came in at ₹4.45L — a month where quality of engagement remained high even as volume eased. March showed one of the most compelling signals in the network: with 63 new trial-class entries, the highest trial proportion of the quarter (67.0% of new members entering as trials), the pipeline for Q2 conversions is exceptionally strong. Notably, March's average conversion span compressed to just 4.4 days — the fastest in the entire network — suggesting Supreme HQ's in-studio experience is generating high-intent buying decisions quickly. The primary strategic focus for Q2 should be converting the 219 Q1 unconverted members, amplifying the WhatsApp and inquiry-on-call channels that are growing rapidly, and extending the "Newcomers 2 For 1" product which nearly doubled in uptake between January and March.

Top 5 Strategic Takeaways

  1. March Trial Pipeline Is Network-Leading at 67%: 63 of March's 94 new entries came as trial class members — the highest trial proportion across all three locations. This is a strong leading indicator of conversion momentum in Q2. A structured trial-to-paid nurture sequence for these 63 members is the highest-priority April action.
  2. 4.4-Day Conversion Span in March — Network Record: This is the fastest buying decision recorded across the entire network in Q1, confirming that Supreme HQ's member experience, instructor quality, and in-studio environment are creating high-urgency purchasing behaviour. This is a differentiator to protect and amplify.
  3. Newcomers 2-For-1 Is the Studio's Fastest-Growing Entry Product: From 31 entries in January to 22 in February and 29 in March — with March showing its second-highest monthly volume — this product is clearly the preferred low-commitment entry point for Bandra-area consumers. It is also the ideal top-of-funnel product for upselling to longer-commitment packages.
  4. WhatsApp and Phone Enquiry Channels Are Rising Fast: Combined WhatsApp + Enquiry on Call accounted for 23 entries in March — up from 10 in January. This signals a shift toward conversational, high-intent acquisition that rewards fast response and personalised follow-up.
  5. Conversion Rate Requires Structural Support: Q1 average conversion rate of 19.1% is below the 25% benchmark. Given that 219 Q1 members remain unconverted and that conversion spans are tightening to under 8 days, the infrastructure for rapid conversion is in place — what is needed now is a systematic offer delivery mechanism within 48 hours of first visit.
