I. Executive Summary

Kenkere House, Bengaluru operates a focused five-instructor class format with 196 total sessions tracked, delivering strong aggregate numbers: ₹8.63L+ total revenue from 1,229 combined attendees. The studio's format is entirely instructor-driven, segmented into two views — by instructor performance and by time slot/day performance. Kajol Kanchan leads with ₹2.5L across 60 sessions, establishing her as the studio's primary revenue anchor.

The most significant structural finding is the late cancel rate disparity between instructor-scheduled classes (Pushyank Nahar at 32.2%, Kajol Kanchan at 29.4%) versus zero-cancel instructors (Shruti Kulkarni, Siddhartha Kusuma at 0%). This 32pp gap in late cancel rates between top and bottom performers suggests that booking friction or session format/timing may be differentially affecting attendance commitment. The 07:15 time slot generates the most absolute attendance at 158 check-ins and ₹1.1L, confirming early morning as Kenkere's peak demand period.

Saturday is the strongest revenue day at ₹1.3L across 29 sessions (avg ₹4.5K/class), while Sunday generates the lowest per-class revenue at ₹3.2K despite reasonable fill rates — suggesting Sunday classes may serve a different audience segment with lower average ticket value. The 90-day strategic priority is to address the high late-cancel instructors while protecting early morning timeslot allocation.

Top 5 Takeaways

  1. Revenue is Strongly Concentrated. Kajol Kanchan and Pushyank Nahar together account for ₹4.2L out of ₹8.6L total — 49% of studio revenue from 2 of 5 instructors. This creates meaningful dependency risk requiring a mitigation strategy.
  2. Zero-Cancel Instructors Are Operationally Superior. Shruti Kulkarni and Siddhartha Kusuma both achieve 0% late cancel rates with 100% show-up rates — their formats run with perfect operational reliability. Their booking patterns should be studied as a model for others.
  3. Evening Late Cancels Dominate. The 17:00 slot (34.5% late cancel) and 18:15 slot (30.6% late cancel) are the studio's highest-risk time slots. Together they represent the bulk of late-cancel leakage, concentrated in the post-work evening window.
  4. Chaitanya Nahar is Under-Monetised. With only 23 sessions and 83 attendees generating ₹68.5K, Chaitanya's format has the highest Rev/Attendee (₹825) of all instructors but the lowest total revenue. Expanding this format could generate outsized returns per-unit.
  5. 07:15 is the Studio's Power Slot. With 158 attendees across 22 sessions (7.2 avg/class) and ₹1.1L revenue, the 07:15 slot outperforms the next best time by 14% in attendance and 13% in revenue. This slot should be protected at all costs in scheduling.
