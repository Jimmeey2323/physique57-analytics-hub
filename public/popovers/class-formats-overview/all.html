Section I — Executive Summary

Across three studio locations — Supreme HQ in Bandra, Kenkere House in Bengaluru, and Kwality House at Kemps Corner — this report analyses class format performance spanning instructor productivity, time-slot fill rates, day-of-week demand patterns, and operational risk signals. The combined portfolio generates an estimated ₹24.5L+ in class revenues across the three studios, with Kwality Kemps Corner emerging as the clear revenue leader at ₹12.5L, followed by Kenkere at ₹7.8L and Supreme at ₹4.2L.

The single most important finding is a structural late-cancellation crisis: Kenkere's two highest-volume instructors (Kajol Kanchan and Pushyank Nahar) together carry 29–32% late cancel rates, and Supreme's Raunak Khemuka has an alarming 53.8% rate, representing direct revenue leakage and operational waste at every studio. The biggest positive signal is Kwality's demonstrated model of high-density, high-quality classes — its instructors average 6–11 attendees per session with strong show-up rates (87–93%), proving a scalable template. The primary risk is overconcentration of attendance in specific instructor-time combinations that, if disrupted, could significantly dent studio revenue.

Top 5 Takeaways

  1. Kwality is the benchmark studio. With ₹12.5L in revenue from 188 sessions and 1,528 attendees, Kwality's ₹818 avg revenue/attendee is the network's operational ceiling. Its Monday class at 7.9 avg/session and ₹6,500/class revenue per session shows what disciplined scheduling delivers.
  2. Late cancellations are the #1 revenue leakage risk. Across all three studios, late cancellation rates on booked-but-not-attending spots range from 10–54%. At ₹600–₹900 estimated per spot, Kenkere alone bleeds an estimated ₹30–45K per month in leakage from Kajol and Pushyank's classes.
  3. Supreme HQ has underutilised slots on Monday and Friday. Monday generates only ₹56.9K from 20 sessions (₹2,845/session) while Tuesday — with fewer sessions — earns ₹88.9K (₹9,900/session). Monday scheduling restructuring could unlock ₹25–35K additional monthly revenue.
  4. The 07:15 time slot is disproportionately valuable at Kenkere. The 07:15 slot runs 22 sessions, 158 attendees and generates ₹1.1L in revenue — the single highest-revenue time slot at ₹5,000/class. Early morning demand is clearly underserved; a second slot at 07:30 or 06:45 could add ₹40–60K monthly.
  5. Instructor fill rates reveal a talent concentration problem at Supreme. The top 2 instructors (Cauveri Vikrant and Vivaran Dhasmana) account for 50% of Supreme's revenue. If either reduces hours, Supreme faces a ≥₹80K/month revenue exposure with no bench depth demonstrated.

Instructor Deep-Dives

Kajol Kanchan — Kenkere House ★ Highest Volume

Kajol is Kenkere's single highest-revenue and highest-attendance instructor at ₹2.5L across 60 sessions and 425 actual attendees — a productivity rate of ₹4,167/session. However, the 29.4% late cancel rate on 602 bookings means that 177 confirmed bookings evaporated pre-class, representing ₹1.06L in theoretically recoverable revenue if cancellation policies were tightened. At a 61% fill rate and average of 7.1 attendees per class, demand is strong but reliability is compromised. The strategic implication is clear: Kajol's audience is willing to book but not always willing to show, suggesting either a high-commitment audience mismatch or a booking-without-intent culture enabled by a lenient cancellation window. A 4-hour late cancellation fee implementation targeting Kajol's classes specifically would likely recover ₹20–35K per month in penalty revenue or force genuine bookings.

Rohan Dahima — Kwality House ★ Highest Rev/Session

Rohan is the network's standout instructor by revenue-per-session at ₹8,800/class across Kwality's main dataset, with 9.7 avg attendees per session and an exceptional 93.4% show-up rate. With only a 6.6% late cancel rate, Rohan's audience is not only booking but attending — this is a power instructor by every operational metric. At Kwality's dataset, Rohan generates ₹1.9L from just 22 sessions; at Supreme, Rohan demonstrates even higher intensity at 11.6 avg/class. The strategic implication is that Rohan's format and scheduling should be studied and replicated. Shifting Rohan to 3–4 additional sessions/week across either Kwality or a cross-studio format could yield ₹35–52K in additional revenue monthly with minimal leakage risk given his track record.

Raunak Khemuka — Supreme HQ ⚠ Critical Concern

Raunak represents Supreme's most acute operational failure: 6 sessions, only 6 actual attendees, 13 bookings, 8% fill rate, and a staggering 53.8% late cancellation rate. Revenue is ₹5,400 total — less than a single strong Rohan Dahima session. This is not a demand problem per se; bookings (13) exceed attendance (6) nearly by double, meaning people are expressing intent but consistently cancelling. At ₹900/attendee in Rev/Attendee, the format itself is not the issue — the late cancel rate is. Continuing to hold 6 sessions/month for this performance level costs the studio scheduling capacity that could be assigned to Cauveri Vikrant or Vivaran Dhasmana, who generate 15–20x more revenue per session. Recommendation: suspend Raunak's current format for 30 days and restructure the slot assignment entirely.

Section IX — Closing Statement

Wins to Celebrate

  • Kwality House is a model studio. With ₹12.5L in revenue, 1,528 attendees, and instructors averaging 7–11 attendees at 87–93% show-up rates, Kwality has demonstrated the highest per-session efficiency in the network. Reshma Sharma's 10.8 avg/class and Rohan Dahima's ₹8,800/session benchmark what disciplined scheduling can achieve.
  • Supreme's premium positioning is working. At ₹856 Rev/Attendee, Supreme commands the network's highest per-person revenue — a validation that the Bandra member is willing to pay more, and that quality is being delivered.
  • Kenkere generates enormous volume from a tight instructor roster. Five instructors across 196 sessions and 1,229 attendees producing ₹7.8L is operationally lean and impressive — though with commensurate concentration risk.

Honest Acknowledgment

  • Late cancellation is a systemic failure, not an isolated data point. The pattern across Kenkere (29–32%) and Supreme (53.8% for one instructor) reflects either a policy gap or a culture that permits casual booking. This is costing the network an estimated ₹1.5–2L per period in real revenue. It will not self-correct.
  • Supreme's Monday schedule is misallocated. Twenty sessions on the studio's weakest day, generating ₹2,845/session against Tuesday's ₹9,900, is a scheduling decision that should have been caught and corrected in a prior cycle.

The Singular Focus for the Next 30 Days

Implement and enforce a late cancellation policy at Kenkere House — specifically targeting classes with >70% booking rates where late cancel exceeds 20%. This single intervention will generate more incremental revenue than any new slot addition or instructor expansion, because it recaptures demand already confirmed by the market. The 12:30 zero-revenue slot investigation should be parallelised.

The 90-Day Vision

If the six quick-win actions in Section VII are executed in sequence, the network should reach ₹28–30L in combined revenue within 90 days — an estimated 15–20% uplift without a single new class format, new instructor hire, or capital outlay. The levers are already in place: the demand exists, the instructors are in the studio, and the scheduling data makes the changes obvious. The work is purely operational.
