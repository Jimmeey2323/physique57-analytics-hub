I. Executive Summary

Kwality House, Kemps Corner is the network's highest-performance studio by fill rate metrics, operating four distinct format segments across a rich instructor and time-slot taxonomy. The studio's flagship "full-schedule" format (Format D-8: by day, 179 sessions, 1,328 attendees, ~₹12L revenue) significantly outperforms its comparable Kenkere/Bandra instructor-only formats, generating ₹6.5–8.8K/class. Monday is the studio's single best day with 35 sessions, 276 attendees, and ₹2.3L total — far above any single day at the other studio locations.

The studio operates a richer format ecosystem with 10–23 instructors depending on the format view, several of which are achieving impressive metrics: Rohan Dahima maintains an 82.1% fill rate across 14 sessions (the network's best consistent fill rate across a significant session volume), while Atulan Purohit's format generates ₹6.7K/class with 64.5% fill. The studio's Kemps Corner-specific format (Format D-4: 14 time slots) shows deep time-based demand with the 10:15 slot delivering 194 attendees across 19 sessions — the highest single-slot attendance in this format segment.

The single most important finding is that Kwality House operates at structurally higher attendance densities than other locations: average attendance per class across the main day-format is 7.4 vs. ~6.0 at Bandra/Bengaluru. This suggests Kemps Corner members are more committed/habitual. The risk is that the Sunday format underperforms dramatically relative to weekdays — Sunday at 3.2 avg/class (Format D-7) is the clear outlier requiring intervention.

Top 5 Takeaways

  1. Monday is the Network's Best Day. Kwality Monday (35 sessions, 276 attendees, ₹2.3L, ₹6.5K/class) outperforms all equivalent-day metrics across all three studio locations. Monday at Kwality is a premium day that should be allocated to top instructors.
  2. Rohan Dahima Leads the Network in Consistent High Fill. 82.1% fill across 14 sessions is exceptional consistency for that session volume. He delivers ₹6.5K/class — among the network's top class-level revenue rates.
  3. 10:15 is Kwality's Power Slot. 194 attendees across 19 sessions (10.2 avg/class) with ₹8.1K/class is the highest single time-slot average in any format across the network. This is extraordinary demand density for a morning mid-week slot.
  4. Sunday is the Studio's Structural Weakness. Format D-7 Sunday averages only 3.2 attendees/class — less than half the Monday average (7.9). Across 15 Sunday sessions, this represents ₹49.5K on 48 attendees: poor volume at moderate revenue per attendee.
  5. Premium Revenue Per Attendee in Smaller Format Slots. Several niche time slots (17:15, 11:30 in D-5) generate ₹1.1–1.2K/attendee — significantly above the ₹750–800 studio average — suggesting underpriced or high-demand premium capacity.
