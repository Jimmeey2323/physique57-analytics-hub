The 15-Point Blueprint for Product Dominance

I. Portfolio Optimization (The Edit)

  1. 1.
    Codify the "Four Pillars" Strategy.

    Rationale: Officially designate `Strength Lab`, `Barre 57`, `Mat 57`, and prime-time `powerCycle` as your core offerings. All scheduling and marketing decisions must prioritize these formats. Your schedule is not a democracy; it's a data-driven meritocracy.

  2. 2.
    Declare War on Underperforming `powerCycle` Slots.

    Rationale: The format is not the problem; its placement is. Immediately cut all `powerCycle` instances with a fill rate below 40%. This single action will eliminate 8 of your worst 12 classes and dramatically boost your studio's average performance.

  3. 3.
    Execute a "Strength Lab Expansion."

    Rationale: All three `Strength Lab` variations (`Push`, `Pull`, `Full Body`) are Top 10 performers in prime time. Replace one of the failing evening `powerCycle` slots (e.g., Thursday 19:15) with an additional `Strength Lab` class.

  4. 4.
    Protect the "Saturday Morning Goldmine."

    Rationale: The 9:00 AM - 11:30 AM block on Saturday is a powerhouse of `Strength Lab`, `Barre 57`, and `Mat 57`. Treat this block as sacred. Do not experiment here. Assign your absolute best instructors to these slots permanently.

  5. 5.
    Sunset or Reposition Niche Formats.

    Rationale: `Studio Cardio Barre` (15% fill rate) and `Studio Recovery` (16.5% fill rate) are not pulling their weight. Replace `Cardio Barre` with a proven winner. Reposition `Recovery` to a Sunday afternoon slot, marketing it as the essential "Weekly Reset."

II. Product Experience & Depth (The Polish)

  1. 6.
    Introduce `Strength Lab 101`.

    Rationale: The format's name can be intimidating. Launch a 4-week workshop or a recurring weekly "Foundations" class on an off-peak afternoon. This creates a safe entry point for new members, feeding your most popular prime-time classes.

  2. 7.
    Brand Your Prime-Time Blocks.

    Rationale: Instead of just listing classes, brand the experience. "Monday Night Strength Block" or "The Saturday Sculpt Session" (for Barre/Mat). This creates a sense of occasion and makes the schedule more intuitive.

  3. 8.
    Develop "Express" Versions of the Pillars.

    Rationale: To capture a potential lunchtime crowd, pilot 45-minute "Express Barre" or "Strength Circuit" classes. This is a low-risk way to add value and utilize mid-day slots that are currently failing with 60-minute formats.

  4. 9.
    Standardize the Core Format Promise.

    Rationale: Ensure that a `Barre 57` class delivers a consistent level of intensity and structure, regardless of the instructor. This builds trust in the product itself, not just in one specific teacher, making your schedule more resilient.

  5. 10.
    Host Technique Workshops for the Four Pillars.

    Rationale: Offer paid, 90-minute workshops like "Master the Kettlebell Swing" for `Strength Lab` or "Deepen Your Practice" for `Mat 57`. This generates ancillary revenue and increases members' confidence and engagement in your core classes.

III. Strategic Growth & Marketing (The Future)

  1. 11.
    Market the Pillars, Not Just the Studio.

    Rationale: Your marketing campaigns should be format-specific. Run a campaign titled "Find Your Strength" focused entirely on `Strength Lab`. Run another called "Sculpt & Lengthen" focused on `Barre` and `Mat`. Target different customer archetypes with the product they want.

  2. 12.
    Create Format-Specific Class Packs.

    Rationale: Offer a "Strength Lab 5-Pack" or a "Barre Burn 10-Pack" at a slight discount. This encourages clients to commit to a specific format, increasing their proficiency and your predictable revenue.

  3. 13.
    Use A/B Testing to Pilot New Formats.

    Rationale: When considering a new class, don't just add it. Temporarily replace one of your bottom-5 performing time slots with the new pilot for one month. If it outperforms the original, it earns a permanent spot. If not, the original returns. No more guesswork.

  4. 14.
    Develop "Level 2" Versions of Popular Formats.

    Rationale: For your most dedicated `Barre` or `Strength` clients, introduce an "Advanced" or "Level 2" class once a week. This creates an aspirational goal, prevents client plateaus, and builds a powerful sense of community among your regulars.

  5. 15.
    Implement a Continuous Format Feedback Loop.

    Rationale: Once per quarter, send a simple one-question survey to your most active members: "What class format do you wish we offered more of?" Use this direct feedback to validate your data and guide the evolution of your product portfolio.
