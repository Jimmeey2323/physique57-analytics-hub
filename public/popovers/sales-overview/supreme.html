Executive Summary & Intelligence

Supreme HQ Bandra exhibits a "Hardening of Revenue". While total sessions dipped slightly (183 in Dec vs 207 in Nov), the revenue floor rose, driven by high-value membership upgrades. The facility is successfully pivoting from a "Volume-based" model to a "Value-based" ecosystem.

Structural Strength

The Annual Lock-In

Dec '25 saw a ₹1.5L injection from 'Annual Unlimited' renewals. This provides immediate cash flow stability and de-risks the Q1 'Resolutionist' period where high churn is typical.

Conversion Alert

Newcomer 2-for-1 Saturation

Revenue from trialists (₹34.5k) has stabilized but hasn't scaled with the holiday surge. There is a potential breakdown in the conversion funnel from 2-for-1 into Unlimited tiers.

Market Pattern

Supreme 'Retail' Momentum

Retail product revenue hit ₹60k in Dec—a significant jump from the ₹20k average in early 2025. Bandra clients are increasingly treating the studio as a lifestyle hub, not just a workout space.

Strategy Gap

Money Credits Dormancy

₹32.4k in Money Credits were utilized in Dec. This indicates a high volume of stored value being burned off, which will require a 'Top-Up' campaign in late Jan.
