📊 Monthly Trend Note

January was the standout month at Supreme HQ, accounting for nearly 45% of total Q1 revenue — reflecting strong New Year demand and membership renewals. February held steady with 203 transactions and healthy member numbers. March shows opportunity: while member count (146) was actually the second-highest of the quarter, revenue per transaction dipped, suggesting a shift toward lower-ticket purchases. A focused upgrade strategy in March — promoting longer-commitment products — could substantially lift Q3 March performance.

Strategic Insights
Key Findings & Opportunities
Data-led observations to guide Supreme HQ's Q2 growth strategy.
🚀
Highest Average Transaction Value
At ₹7,351 per transaction, Supreme HQ commands the highest average ticket size across the entire network — a clear reflection of its premium brand positioning and the quality of client conversion by the sales team.
🌟
Strongest New Member Acquisition
170 new member transactions — the highest across all locations — demonstrates that Supreme HQ's market positioning and outreach strategy is effectively bringing fresh clientele into the Physique 57 ecosystem.
💰
Memberships Dominate Revenue Mix
At 53.4%, memberships account for the majority of revenue — higher than any other location. This creates a strong recurring revenue foundation that insulates the business from month-to-month variability.
📈
Newcomer Revenue Converts Well
₹2.26L from 120 Newcomer Special transactions indicates a healthy intro-to-commitment funnel. With structured follow-through from intro members into full memberships or packages, this pipeline can become even more lucrative.
🛍️
Retail Has Growth Headroom
₹69,691 from 81 retail transactions is the lowest retail revenue across full-service locations. Given the premium clientele and high footfall, there is meaningful potential to grow retail through curated collections and in-studio presentation.
🤝
Well-Distributed Sales Team
Four members of the sales team collectively contributed over ₹32L in attributed revenue. The distribution is healthy, with no over-reliance on a single individual — a sign of team depth and organisational resilience.
Strategic Recommendations
Q2 2026 Action Plan
Targeted actions to accelerate performance at Supreme HQ in the next quarter.
  • 1
    Convert Newcomers into Long-Term Members: With 120 Newcomer Special purchases, Supreme HQ has the most active intro funnel in the network. A structured 30/60-day follow-up process — personalised outreach, milestone celebrations, and upgrade incentives — will significantly lift conversion rates into high-value memberships and packages.
  • 2
    Protect the March Revenue Gap: March revenue fell to ₹9.26L versus January's ₹20L. Launching a "Spring Into Fitness" campaign in late February — featuring pre-booked March packages, multi-month memberships, or pair-and-save offers — can pre-fill the March revenue pipeline before the dip sets in.
  • 3
    Invest in Retail Experience: With 363 unique members visiting in Q1 and the highest average spend per visit, retail is a natural extension of the member experience. Investing in in-studio merchandise displays, new product launches, and seasonal capsule collections can grow retail from ₹70K to well above ₹2L per quarter.
  • 4
    Leverage the Premium Positioning: Supreme HQ has the highest ATV in the portfolio. This is an opportunity to introduce exclusive, high-ticket offerings — VIP memberships, premium training bundles, or corporate wellness packages — that only this location is positioned to command.
  • 5
    Expand Private Class Offerings: Private class revenue of ₹20,794 from just 5 transactions is minimal. Given Supreme HQ's high-income catchment area, targeted outreach to members for private and semi-private sessions represents a high-margin, low-infrastructure growth lever.
