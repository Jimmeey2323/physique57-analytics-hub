I. Executive Summary

Supreme HQ, Bandra is Kwality's highest-revenue single location, generating ₹25.99L in Q1 2026 across 675 class sessions — making it 60% more productive than Kenkere House by revenue despite only 26% more sessions. The location posted its strongest month in March 2026 with ₹10.21L, representing 41.9% growth over February and a 42% increase from January. The headline insight is that Studio FIT is the most efficient format at this location — 65.6% fill rate with the highest class average (6.6) — yet it runs only 96 sessions versus PowerCycle's 200. PowerCycle dominates by volume (200 sessions, ₹7.95L revenue) but operates at only 38.2% fill, making it the biggest single opportunity for yield improvement in the network. Top trainer Atulan Purohit averages 7.6 members per class — almost twice the location's 5.3 average — signalling exceptional member pull that is under-leveraged by current scheduling. Late cancellations are significant (628 total) but remained relatively stable across the quarter, suggesting this location has a structural late-cancel rate rather than an accelerating one. Saturday and Thursday are peak days, with Sunday showing standout average performance (5.6 class avg) against relatively few sessions.

  1. Supreme HQ is the network's revenue flagship. ₹25.99L in Q1 at an average of ₹8.66L/month — more than double Kenkere House and 73% of Kwality House's output despite similar session counts. Revenue per session of ₹3,851 (March) is 5% higher than Kenkere House.
  2. PowerCycle is a high-volume, low-efficiency anchor. With 200 sessions and only 38.2% fill, PowerCycle occupies prime slots at below-optimal utilisation. At 65.6% fill (FIT's rate), PowerCycle alone would generate an additional ₹3.5L in quarterly revenue.
  3. Atulan Purohit is the location's most valuable trainer asset. At 7.6 class average across 87 sessions, he generates ₹4.23L — second highest by revenue — but with the highest per-session efficiency. A targeted schedule increase of 20 sessions/month for Purohit could yield ₹35–50K incremental revenue.
  4. Barre 57 punches above its session count. With 183 sessions generating ₹6.85L (5.5 class avg, 39.5% fill), Barre 57 is the second format by revenue. Its 152 late cancels are high but stable — an enforcement opportunity, not an acceleration risk.
  5. Sunday sessions massively under-supply demonstrated demand. Sunday posts a 5.6 class average on only 69 sessions — matching Thursday (5.9) with 35% fewer slots. An additional 20 Sunday sessions would represent ~₹70K in untapped quarterly revenue.
