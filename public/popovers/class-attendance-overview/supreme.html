Executive Summary: A Tale of Two Products

The November data for Supreme HQ doesn't just show a scheduling issue; it reveals a fundamental product schism. On one hand, you have a portfolio of high-demand, cult-favorite classes like `Studio FIT` that are wildly successful. On the other, you are dedicating prime schedule real estate and payroll to a product, `Studio Cardio Barre`, that your members have unequivocally rejected. This isn't speculation; it's a verdict delivered across multiple trainers, time slots, and days of the week.

The path forward is clear and non-negotiable: a strategic overhaul of the class menu. Success hinges on surgically removing the underperforming assets and reallocating those resources to amplify your proven winners. This is an exercise in product management, not just studio management.

The Deep Dive: An Unfiltered Analysis

The Champions: What's Working Brilliantly

Your studio has a core of undeniable hits. These are your crown jewels.

  • `Studio FIT` is Your Superstar: This format is a revenue engine, accounting for over 15% of total check-ins from its top slots alone. With instructors like Mrigakshi Jaiswal and Vivaran Dhasmana achieving 100% fill rates, and Atulan Purohit commanding near-90% attendance, you have a certified blockbuster.
  • Morning Slots are Golden: The 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM weekday and Saturday morning slots are your most valuable real estate, consistently producing top-performing classes across `FIT`, `Barre 57`, and `Mat 57`.
  • Star Power is Real: Instructors like Atulan Purohit, Reshma Sharma, and Mrigakshi Jaiswal are proven draws. Their names are assets that should be leveraged.

The Liabilities: What's Draining Resources

Let's be candid about what's holding the studio back.

  • `Studio Cardio Barre` is a Financial Black Hole: This format is not just underperforming; it's a failed product. It accounts for a staggering 35% of your Bottom 20 classes while generating less than 5% of total revenue. Keeping it on the schedule is an active choice to lose money.
  • The 12.3% Cancellation Rate is a Cry for Help: This high rate is a direct consequence of a weak offering. Members book a class they're unenthusiastic about, and then cancel. This churn creates uncertainty and prevents truly interested members from booking, directly harming your fill rate and revenue.
  • Inconsistent Trainer/Format Fit: Certain trainers repeatedly appear on the Bottom 20 list for specific formats. This suggests not a lack of skill, but a misalignment. An amazing `Barre` instructor may not have the right vibe for `powerCycle`, leading to member disappointment and empty slots.

The 15-Point Blueprint for Market Dominance

I. Product & Schedule Overhaul (The Foundation)

  1. Strategic Sunset of `Cardio Barre`: Immediately remove the entire `Cardio Barre` line from your schedule.

    Rationale: Data shows a complete product rejection. This single move frees up ~10-15 weekly slots for profitable classes, instantly boosting your average fill rate potential.

  2. Clone the `Studio FIT` Success: Add at least three more `Studio FIT` classes per week into the now-vacant prime slots (e.g., Friday 19:15, a weekday AM slot). Assign your star `FIT` instructors to these new slots.

    Rationale: The most basic business principle: sell more of what people are eagerly buying.

  3. Curate the Daily Flow: Redesign the schedule to create a clear daily identity.
    • Mon/Tues: High-energy starts. AM `FIT`/`Mat`, PM `FIT`/`Barre`.
    • Wed: Mid-week grind. Pilot a new "Strength" class here.
    • Thurs/Fri: Results-focused. AM `Mat`/`FIT`, PM `powerCycle`/`Barre`.
    • Sat/Sun: The "Best Of" collection. Back-to-back blocks of your most popular `FIT`, `Barre`, and `powerCycle` classes.
  4. Rebrand & Relocate `Studio Recovery`: The concept is good, the execution is failing. Rename it "Pro-Level Stretch & Mobility" and schedule it on a Sunday afternoon, marketing it as the essential reset for the week ahead.

    Rationale: The current 13% fill rate shows it's in the wrong place at the wrong time. Repositioning targets a specific need-state.

  5. Pilot "Supreme Strength": The success of `FIT` indicates a demand for effective, strength-based training. Launch a 6-week pilot program for a true strength training class in a prime evening slot.

    Rationale: Product innovation based on existing, proven demand is the smartest way to grow your offering.

II. Talent & Performance Maximization (The Accelerant)

  1. Implement Tiered Instructor Roles: Formally recognize instructors like Atulan, Mrigakshi, and Vivaran as "Lead Instructors."

    Rationale: This creates a career path, justifies higher pay for top performers, and allows them to mentor other instructors.

  2. Mandate Instructor "Specializations": Assign trainers to the one or two formats where they demonstrably excel. Stop assigning instructors to classes where they underperform.

    Rationale: This ensures quality control and consistency, directly combatting the high cancellation rate driven by member disappointment.

  3. Launch a Performance-Based Bonus System: Introduce a quarterly bonus for instructors who consistently maintain an average fill rate above a certain threshold (e.g., 70%).

    Rationale: Aligns instructor goals with business goals. They will become your best marketers.

  4. Create "Signature Slots": Brand specific classes with the instructor, e.g., "FIT Riot with Atulan" on Fridays.

    Rationale: Builds scarcity and hype, turning a simple class into a can't-miss event and deepening member loyalty to the instructor and studio.

  5. Host Internal Workshops: Have your Lead Instructors run workshops for the rest of the team on class programming, playlisting, and engagement.

    Rationale: Levels up the quality of your entire teaching team, making your schedule more resilient.

III. Growth & Member Retention (The Flywheel)

  1. Market the Solution, Not Just the Class: Shift marketing language. Instead of "Try Studio FIT," use "Get Real Results with Studio FIT, Our Most Popular Class."

    Rationale: Sells the outcome and leverages social proof to attract new and existing members to your best products.

  2. Launch "FIT Foundations" Workshop: Create a paid, 4-week introductory workshop for `Studio FIT`.

    Rationale: De-risks the class for beginners and creates a dedicated, profitable pipeline of new clients for your most popular format.

  3. Implement a Stricter Cancellation Policy for Prime Slots: For classes that are consistently waitlisted, shorten the cancellation window from 12 hours to 8 hours.

    Rationale: This will discourage casual cancellations for high-demand spots and give waitlisted members a better chance to get in, improving overall fill rates.

  4. Survey with Intent: After implementing changes, send a survey asking, "What ONE class would you love to see more of on the schedule?"

    Rationale: You've shown you listen by cutting the bad stuff. Now, let your loyal members guide your next growth phase.

  5. Create Themed Class Packs: Introduce a "FIT 10-Pack" or a "Weekend Warrior Pass" (good for Sat/Sun classes only).

    Rationale: Drives revenue and encourages members to commit to the specific class ecosystems you are intentionally building.
