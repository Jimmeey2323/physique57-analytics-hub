I. Executive Summary

Kenkere House is on a strong and accelerating growth trajectory, posting its highest revenue month ever in March 2026 at ₹6.81L — a 40.8% surge over February and a 49.3% increase since January. Total quarterly revenue reached ₹16.2L across 537 class sessions, driven by a 48.1% lift in check-ins from January to March. The single most important number is the March check-in count of 1,149 — the clearest signal that member demand is genuinely rising, not simply a pricing effect. Studio Barre 57 is the engine of this growth, accounting for 45% of all revenue (₹7.18L) while Studio FIT punches above its weight with a 59.9% fill rate, the highest of any format. The critical risk flags are the late cancellation rate spiking to 291 in March — a 106% month-on-month increase — and the fact that two trainers (Kajol Kanchan and Pushyank Nahar) hold all the cancellation load, suggesting a structural dependency. Weekday evenings and Saturday mornings are clear demand peaks, and underutilised Sunday and Wednesday slots represent 30–40% unrealised capacity. If growth continues at the current slope, April revenue is projected at ₹7.4–7.9L.

  1. Revenue acceleration is real and structural. Monthly revenue jumped from ₹4.56L (Jan) → ₹4.84L (Feb) → ₹6.81L (Mar), an overall 49.3% gain in 8 weeks. The March inflection is not a pricing anomaly — it is underpinned by 1,149 check-ins, the highest single-month attendance on record for this location.
  2. Late cancellations are a growing revenue leak. Late cancels doubled from 127 (Jan) to 291 (Mar) — a 129% increase. At an estimated ₹550 per class slot, March alone represents ~₹1.6L in potential leakage, or 23% of March revenue left on the table.
  3. Studio Barre 57 dominates but concentration risk exists. Barre 57 delivers 45% of revenue from 225 sessions — but its fill rate of 42% signals significant remaining capacity. Revenue concentration above 40% from one format is a business risk if demand shifts.
  4. Studio Recovery and Trainer's Choice are hidden gems. Recovery posts a 66.4% fill rate and Trainer's Choice 52.3% with a class average of 6.8 — both above location average. These high-engagement formats are under-scheduled (just 25 sessions each in 3 months).
  5. Weekend demand outpaces weekday supply. Saturday is the #1 day by check-ins (524) and revenue (₹2.96L) yet has only 88 sessions — fewer than Thursday (84 sessions, 462 check-ins). Sunday is the most underserved day with only 52 sessions but a strong 5.6 class average.
