The Bandra Doctrine: A Path Forward From This Mess

The path forward is clear, simple, and involves a strategic tool known as "common sense." Here are the new commandments for Supreme HQ.

Commandment 1: The Law of Profitable Allocation

Problem: Non-Barre formats are occupying valuable real estate and payroll hours while contributing virtually nothing.
Solution: Any trainer who records a revenue-per-session under ₹1,000 in ANY format will be sent to a mandatory "Barre Re-education Camp." Effective immediately, 50% of the Strength Lab and PowerCycle class slots are canceled and replaced with Barre. The strong will survive. The weak will teach pliés.

Commandment 2: The Richard D'Costa Protocol

Problem: A single class generating ₹2.56 is not a rounding error; it is a declaration of financial war against this company.
Solution: We are framing that ₹2.56 receipt and hanging it in the staff room as a monument to what we must never become. The trainer responsible will be required to write a 500-word essay on the principles of commerce. Furthermore, we are implementing a "Minimum Revenue Threshold." Any class that fails to generate a baseline revenue will be automatically flagged, and the studio space will be fumigated to remove the bad financial juju.

Commandment 3: Operation Find the Money

Problem: The data is an unmitigated disaster.
Solution: We are hiring an intern. Their sole job will be to sit in a dark room with the person who crafted this data dump (looking at you, Jimmeey Gondaa) and manually reconstruct reality. We cannot make intelligent decisions based on spreadsheets that look like a ransom note. This is priority one. We need to know if the money is missing, or if it was never there to begin with.
