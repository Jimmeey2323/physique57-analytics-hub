I. Executive Summary

Kwality House, Kemps Corner is the largest and highest-revenue location in the Kwality network, generating ₹40.42L in Q1 2026 across 850 sessions — 55% more revenue than Supreme HQ and 150% more than Kenkere House. The standout single data point in the entire network is Studio Mat 57 at this location: a 10.4 class average — nearly double any other format across all three locations. With 64 sessions and ₹4.65L in revenue, Mat 57 is operating at capacity pressure, suggesting an urgent expansion opportunity. The location's Q1 trajectory — ₹12.85L (Jan), ₹12.59L (Feb), ₹14.98L (Mar) — shows a dip in February followed by a strong March recovery (+19.0% MoM), while class averages held steady at 6.1–6.8 throughout, indicating stable member retention rather than boom-bust dynamics. Rohan Dahima is the network's single most impactful trainer: averaging 8.4 check-ins per class across 107 sessions, generating ₹7.21L — more than the entire Kenkere House location earns in an average month. Late cancellations remain elevated (707 total) and grew 47.6% in March — a pattern that now demands urgent policy intervention. The network's richest product mix (10 distinct formats) is both a strength (broad member appeal) and a scheduling complexity risk.

  1. Kwality House is the network's flagship — and Mat 57 is the crown jewel. A 10.4 class average is extraordinary in boutique fitness — it means every session has a waitlist culture. This is the rarest kind of supply-demand imbalance: too much demand, not enough supply. Immediate capacity expansion is the correct response.
  2. Rohan Dahima is the network's most valuable trainer. 8.4 class average over 107 sessions (the highest volume of any trainer at this location) generating ₹7.21L in a single quarter. He alone drives 17.8% of this location's revenue. His schedule should be protected and expanded with urgency.
  3. Strength Lab formats are a differentiator not being maximised. Three Strength Lab variants (Pull, Full Body, Push) combined generate ₹5.21L from 177 sessions — and all three post fill rates above 58%, with Pull reaching 71.9%. No other location in the network offers this format; it is a genuine competitive moat.
  4. February dip warrants investigation. Revenue fell ₹260K (−2.0%) MoM in February while check-ins also declined slightly (1,743 → 1,693). In a growing network context, this is an anomaly — possibly tied to fewer sessions (284 → 274) or a calendar effect (shorter month). The March recovery (+19%) suggests the dip was temporary, but the root cause should be confirmed.
  5. Late cancellations surged 47.6% in March and are a network-wide priority. 271 cancels in March (from 183 in Feb) represents the second-fastest MoM spike after Kenkere House. Combined, Kwality House and Kenkere House lost 559 class slots in March alone — a systemic problem requiring an immediate network-wide late cancel fee.
