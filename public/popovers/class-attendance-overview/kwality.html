Strategic Insights & Analysis
Revenue Engine: Back Body Blaze Strategic Win
Anisha Shah's "Back Body Blaze" on Wednesdays at 09:00 is the undisputed powerhouse class for the month, achieving an average fill rate of 77.5% (High: 80% on Jan 17). This format significantly outperforms others, generating approximately ₹960 per head, the highest revenue per attendee metric observed.
Recommendation: Protect this slot aggressively. Consider adding a second session of "Back Body Blaze" on a different day (e.g., Monday or Friday) at the same time to capture overflow demand, as other formats at this time perform significantly worse.
Critical PM Slot Underperformance Performance Alert
Evening sessions (post 17:00) are struggling significantly across all formats. Anisha's 17:45 "Studio Barre 57" averages only 32% fill rate, while Atulan's 18:00 "Cardio Barre Plus" averages a mere 22% fill rate. This creates a substantial revenue gap between AM and PM operations.
Recommendation: Conduct a survey to identify barriers to PM attendance (traffic vs. preference). Pilot swapping PM schedules to "Express" formats (30-45 mins) or shifting popular AM formats to PM slots to test product-fit vs. time-slot preference.
Trainer-Format Mismatch Detected Talent Optimization
Data reveals a clear mismatch for Trainer Atulan Purohit with the "Back Body Blaze" format. On Jan 4, Atulan achieved only a 20% fill rate for this class, compared to Anisha's consistent 75%+ performance. Conversely, Atulan excels in standard AM Barre 57 (67% fill).
Recommendation: Immediately reassign Atulan from "Back Body Blaze" to formats where he shows strength (Standard Barre/Express). Allocate niche formats like Back Body Blaze exclusively to trainers with proven track records (Anisha) to maximize yield.
The "Studio FIT" Anomaly Efficiency
The "Studio FIT" format (Wed 11:30) is underperforming with an average fill rate of 35%. It sits in a mid-day "dead zone" where attendance drops off before picking up for evening classes. The generic naming may also be less appealing than result-oriented titles.
Recommendation: Rename "Studio FIT" to something more specific (e.g., "HIIT Fusion" or "Power Flow") and consider moving this slot to 07:00 AM or 19:00 PM where demand for intensity might be higher.
High Retention in Membership Classes Client Behavior
Analysis of Anisha's high-performing classes shows a high correlation with Membership usage (e.g., 12 members in Back Body Blaze). Classes with higher membership counts (vs. Single Class/Package) show more consistent attendance patterns and lower volatility week-over-week.
Recommendation: Double down on converting Single Class users to Memberships by targeting the users of consistently full AM classes with "Lock in this rate" offers, specifically around the "Mat 57" and "Back Body Blaze" formats.
Foundations Classes Not Converting Operational Alert
"Studio Foundations" sessions are chronically under-attended, often seeing only 1-3 attendees (e.g., Atulan's Jan 2 class: 3 attendees; Anisha's Jan 20 class: 2 attendees). This indicates a bottleneck in the new client funnel.
Recommendation: Re-evaluate the marketing lead-in for Foundations. Consider integrating Foundations as a 15-min add-on to popular classes or moving it to weekend slots where new users might have more availability, rather than mid-week mornings.
AM vs PM Revenue Disparity Forecast
There is a projected 60/40 split in revenue potential favoring AM slots. If PM performance is not corrected, the studio is effectively paying for 12 hours of operational overhead to generate revenue in only 4 peak hours (8 AM - 12 PM).
Recommendation: Investigate hybrid scheduling or reduced PM capacity hours. Alternatively, target corporate partnerships for PM slots to fill the current void left by individual members.
Monday Morning Mat 57 Spike Hidden Pattern
Anisha's Monday 08:30 "Studio Mat 57" shows a distinct spike to 70% capacity on Jan 15, outperforming her other sessions. This suggests Monday is a key "Reset Day" for clients looking to start the week strong.
Recommendation: Promote Monday AM sessions heavily as "Monday Motivation" specials. Consider duplicating the "Mat 57" format on Monday evenings to test if the motivation carries over or if it is strictly a morning phenomenon.
Anisha's Premium Positioning Talent Optimization

Cardio Barre Plus Struggles Format Analysis

Capacity Wastage on High Demand Revenue Optimization

Recommendation: Implement a "Premium Booking" fee for high-demand classes (Back Body Blaze) allowing members to guarantee a spot for a small surcharge, or implement a strict waitlist policy that charges for late cancellations to reduce no-shows.

Package Usage > Single Classes Client Behavior
In high-performing classes, Package usage exceeds Single Class usage by a ratio of 3:1. This indicates that the core loyal audience buys in bulk, while drop-ins are sporadic and low-yield.
